Fossil Threads in the Web of Life

February
Scott Sampson

What’s 75 million years old and brand spanking new? A teenage Utahceratops! Come to the Saroyan, armed with your best dinosaur roar, when Scott Sampson, Research Curator at the Utah Museum of Natural History, steps to the podium. Sampson’s research has focused on the ecology and evolution of late Cretaceous dinosaurs and he has conducted fieldwork in a number of countries in Africa.

Scott Sampson is a Canadian-born paleontologist who received his Ph.D. in zoology from the University of Toronto. Following graduation in 1993, he spent a year working at the American Museum of Natural History in New York City, followed by five years as an assistant professor of anatomy at the New York College of Osteopathic Medicine.

In addition to his museum and laboratory-based studies, Sampson has conducted paleontological work in Zimbabwe, South Africa, and Madagascar, as well as in the United States. He is the author of numerous scientific and popular articles and the book Dinosaur Odyssey: Fossil Threads in the Web of Life.

Sampson currently serves as Executive Director of the California Academy of Sciences, where he continues to inspire audiences to reconnect with nature and explore the story of life on Earth.
